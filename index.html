The Tholakele Kidz vegetable garden project aimed to promote healthy living for children from a very young age. They were involved in every step of the process and to teach them to gain ownership of their healthy eating choices. Gardening can help children to better understand the process of gardening and where their food comes from. The project also provided a wonderful opportunity for children to learn skills that will prepare them for the future and help them to be better equipped for life. Their motor skills will be developed as gardening requires children to practice their fine motor skills. Planting seeds, scooping dirt and watering plants all help to improve motor skills, which will help them to improve their concentration and learning capabilities. Through this, children learned the importance of taking care of their environment. It is shown that gardening can heighten all senses in children. They can feel the dirt, flowers, fruit, leaves and seeds, see the vibrant colours and sizes of plants, hear the sounds of leaves rustling and fruit/ vegetables being picked and smell the fruit, flowers and fresh herbs. Children also learn the importance of responsibility and accountability, they learn that it is part of their job to take care of their vegetables in order for them to be healthy.
